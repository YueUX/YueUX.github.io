Connect you with foodie buddies in the community.
Enjoy your favorite food,
...and time with the food-makers.

Fooriend is a concept app that connects people with other foodie buddies in the community, especially individual inhabitants. Imagine finding someone nearby whose cooking makes your mouth watering and joining a potluck-style meetup with them.

This is an individual school project that involves problem finding,  ideation, comparative analysis, user flow, paper prototype, Hi-fi mockups and prototypes completed in 12 weeks.

1. Problem

Living alone and cooking by oneself is not at all an interesting story.
Scroll down to see how.
“Cooking that small amount I'd rather skip it”

“I'm tired of eating my own sandwiches every day”
Cooking for oneself is too hard.
Cooking the small amount for oneself usually inhibits people's motivation to cook. People get tired of eating the same several dishes that one can cook all the time.
Can't find other foodie buddies.
Current cooking app are not satisfying. Food lovers in the neighborhood do not feel like connected with each other whom they can enjoy food and exchanging recipes with.
“There is no one I can exchange recipes with”

"I wish to tickle my taste buds."

Cooking and dining alone and the feeling of disconnected with people alike is causing some level of depression. Behind the scene, is actually a Social Need. So, my design aims to restore the bonds between these solitude inhabitants and reduce the hardness of cooking and dining all by themselves everyday.

2. Solution

Eating good food with friends is one of the joys of life. To connect people who seek the pleasure of various kinds of food and who have the hidden social need of eating, my design encourages and facilitates potluck meetup in the community. Potluck style meetup is a good idea with distributed food preparations among a group of people where they come to dine together and have good food and conversations.

Design Philosophy of this app

1
               

Food serves as a great bridge for people to be connected. Life story sharing, business collaborations, emotion catharsis...We can never image the potentiality of food in people's social engagement.

2
               

As “screen time” has taken place much of “face time” in people’s social communication, my design aims to encourage in-person interaction by creating a online platform for people to find FOORIENDS offline.
My solution is a mobile app that matches individuals by pictures of their cooking and provides a channel for them to form a potluck meetup group with people connected. It reinforces the connection between unacquainted individuals, and at the end of the day help to create a stress-free and fun food meetup.

4. User Journey

User can browse the picture of dishes others cooked and their recipes and they like or dislike this person's cooking. Mutual likes will automatically link the two. After that, users can propose potluck events with the ones they match.
