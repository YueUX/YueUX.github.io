With 10+ hours a week of four months, I participated in this CHI project as a UX researcher and prototyper. At early stage, I actively involved in need-seeking and problem research, conducted three in-depth interviews with pregnant women and one parental guru. Later I led the solution-finding process based on the user storyboard. On the design side, I created the high-fi prototype and poster.

1. Problem Defining



To first understand the challenges faced by pregnant women, we conduct 7 contextual inquires, including 5 pregnant women in their 2nd and 3rd trimester and 2 gynecologists, to explore the challenges of their pregnancy.

By creating Affinity Wall and 7 contextual inquiry with pregnant women, we concluded the main challenges during pregnancy. We decided to focus on nausea and vomiting because they have been reported consistently across all the interviewees as one serious and unsolvable problem during their pregnancy.

2. Research


In order to get a more comprehensive understandings of the symptom, causation, solution, and impact of NVP, we conducted a series of 9 in-depth interviews and selectively review 20 research articles. In each interview, the participant was asked for his or her experience and coping mechanism for NVP.

Research Insights:
  · 1) NVP has predictable daily patterns as well as unpredictable triggers.
  · 2) Non-pharmaceutical approaches to relieve NVP were preferred and predominantly used.
  · 3) NVP has a significant impact both on pregnant women’s productivity in the work space as well as the overall quality of life.
  · 4) Although research showed that social and emotional support would help relieve NVP.

3. IDEATION

Based on our understandings of the characteristics of NVP and the challenges faced by our interviewees, we have generated the following design requirements.

Simple recording action: 
record NVP related symptoms and foods is complicated and frequent recording would further exhaust pregnancy women.
Reduction of time
using the system: According to our interview, long-term use of screen devices
such as mobile phones and laptops will trigger feelings of nausea and vomiting.
Avoid interfaces
that would become associated with feelings of nausea and vomiting, espcially image and words associated with food triggers.
Avoid active notification: a reminder of an upcoming nausea or vomiting episode will
frustrate the pregnant woman and possibly trigger or worsen her symptoms.

Following our design requirements, we have designed Dot-it system to assist pregnant women in managing NVP:



       RECORD
A simple method are designed to record NVP either on the acupressure band and the mobile app. After users push the button of the acupressure band, the device will send the data to the mobile device and then users can later go to the application to report their symptoms.







       EXPLORE
Based on the previous personal records, Dot-it interprets the data in visualized information where mothers can explore the trend and pattern in terms of hour, day, week, and month.

                RE-SCHEDULE

A simple method are designed to record NVP either on the acupressure band and the mobile app. After users push the button of the acupressure band, the device will send the data to the mobile device and then users can later go to the application to report their symptoms.







          CONNECTING WITH PARTNER

Research suggests that social support can help pregnant women relieve their symptom of NVP. Through our interviews, we noticed that women’s partners are willing to help, but they lack information and knowledge. As a result, our application allows partners to view the data history of pregnant women and help them record NVP symptoms by both installing our application. The partner will receive notification of incoming episodes, so they can support pregnant women as well as take actions in advance. 



4. Prototype & Usability Test 


We developed low-fidelity prototype and asked 8 pregnant women to test it by discovering NVP patterns via the visualized history data, scheduling a meeting in a symptom-free time, and recording NVP symptoms. Then we created high-fidelity prototype and carried out usability test on focusing on gathering data of the feasibility of the application as well as the partners’ feedback on the notification and co-editing feature.

SOME RESULTS OF USABILITY TEST:
   a) Users liked the visualization of history data which was intuitive, thus users were able to discover their NVP pattern via the representation.
   b) Some work flows and interface designs confused users, for example, the color mapping of the recording page should be consistent with the color in the exploring page.
   c) Users liked to record and relieve their symptoms via acupressure band.
